Russia vs Saudi Arabia

Luzhniki Stadium
  • Thu, 14 Jun
  • 16:00 +01:00
  • Group A

Preview

Host nation Russia take on Saudi Arabia in the opening match of the 2018 FIFA World Cup at the Luzhniki Stadium in Moscow.

The sides have only met once before, with the Saudis winning a 4-2 friendly encounter in October 1993. 

The FIFA World Cup will get underway in the Russian capital with the tournament hosts coming up against an unfancied Saudi Arabia.

The Green Falcons will be playing in their first World Cup match in 12 years while they have only progressed past the group stages once – in 1994, when they were eliminated at the last 16 stage.

This will be Russia’s fourth World Cup since the break up of the Soviet Union yet have failed to progress past the groups in each of their previous attempts, including last time out in Brazil.

Stanislav Cherchesov’s side will be strong favourites for this encounter and will believe they must get off to a winning start if they are to qualify from a group which includes Egypt and Uruguay.
